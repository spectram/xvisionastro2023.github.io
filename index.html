This Capacity Building Workshop aims to provide a unique platform for students and young researchers from developing nations to learn how to analyze X-ray data from missions like XMM-Newton, NICER and NuSTAR. The teams of experts include a number of renowned senior lecturers from various fields. The hands-on sessions will be led by the respective instrument team members. The Workshop aims also at creating local know-how enabling a generation of young researchers to use the future X-ray observing facilities like XRISM, ATHENA, Arcus etc. Needless to say, these observatories will undoubtedly trigger an entirely new era in X-ray astronomy and our understanding of the energetic universe.

This Workshop is the first of its kind where the International Astronomical Union (IAU) and Committee on Space Research (COSPAR) are jointly supporting this initiative for capacity building in Astronomy. This workshop is also supported generously by the South African Gamma-ray Astronomy Programme (SA-GAMMA) and Centre for Space Research, North-West University, Potchefstroom, National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) and European Space Agency (ESA) in various forms.

There is no registration fee for this workshop. Limited financial support for travel is also available. The participants with accompanying person(s) should send us an email on xrayvision2023@gmail.com to get accomodation arranged by the organizers. However, the participant will be responsible for the related expenses (travel, accommodation, etc.). More information can be obtained over email.

The IAU I-HOW and COSPAR Capacity Building Workshops have a rich history of enriching young researchers across the globe with the technical and scientific capabilities in a diverse area of Space Science and Astronomy. These short training cum teaching programmes are very effective and provide ample opportunities for collaborative research with experts and local groups. This particular Workshop also aims to highlight a number of research programmes, in line with the main research theme, being conducted in South Africa and to explore new possibilities of Pan-Africa collaborations.

These Capacity Building Workshops also promote cultural exchange between participants through various activities after the normal workshop hours.

The Lectures

The Lectures and hands-on sessions shall revolve around the following major scientific areas, but are by no means limited to:

  • The ionized outflows from black-hole objects
  • The spin, mass and nature of accretion disk in Stellar mass Black holes and AGNs
  • The X-ray emission from Blazars and connection with the TeV emission from jets
  • Thermal X-ray emission from Galaxy Clusters
  • X-ray emission from Pulsars
  • Multi-Messenger Astronomy and Transients
  • High energy Astronomy in South Africa
